    A little about myself

    I'm from Belfast, Northern Ireland. Born & bred. A great city but I didn't see much opportunity for learning cyber-security at Universities or on Apprenticeships here, so I decided to head to good ol' Scotland to study Ethical Hacking at Abertay University. Which is where I am now, in my third year. I'm very passionate about cyber security and loving every new area or technique I come across.

    I went to the Belfast Metropolitan College to study cyber security in 2017. I learned that I loved the security field achieving D*DD with my extended diploma. There was, and still is, an overwhelming amount of knowledge to cover in this industry but I've found that's a requirement for being in it. Ever-changing.

    Raspberry Pi's!!!

    In 2015 I came across a strange device called the Raspberry Pi 1. Described as a credit card sized miniture computer, I was fascinated with how much such a tiny thing could do. I started off by creating the common projects such as a home media center using OSMC, retropi game emulator and a desktop environment with Raspbian OS.


    To venture into more uncommon grounds I attempted to make a jukebox with the raspberry Pi 3 model B using a breadboard, header/connecter cables, speaker, LCD screen and more. It looked cool, but sadly I still wasn't experieced enough to finish this project. When I got more experienced I attempted to install kali linux on the raspberry 3 to test out the popular pentest OS. However, I found it to not run very well having a lot of issues getting it setup. Also, if I remember correctly it didn't have half the tools available from the regular OS, so I decided to abandon this project back then.


    I've actually recently got a raspbery pi 4 and successfully got kali linux on it now!, and finding it to be so much better with the stronger hardware it has. With all the tools too! (except BurpSuite :(, but hopefully going to get it working with an old 32bit-ARM version).

    Rubik's Cubes


    I've always loved puzzles, but the Rubik's cubes are my favourite. I started off like everyone else with the original 3x3. It was tough, however it was very fun. I spent 3 months turning that thing every way my brain could think, before I finally had a basic understanding of the goal and method to solve it. I couldn't get past the middle stage, and finally gave in. A quick Google search and I found the offical algorithms to solve it. So I followed them, and the satisfaction on finally seeing all those muddled colours in place was something else.


    I instantly fell in love with these wee puzzles, and have went on to get the 2x2, 3x1 (A pen haha) and 4x4 (just solved recently). Next is the big 5x5.
